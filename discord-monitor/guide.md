6 - I sometimes see this next to news and scanner pops " ~  1 for 16  R/S  Jan. 04 " - what does it mean?

This is when a company has changed the number of existing shares into either less, with a reverse split (R/S) or more shares, with a forward split (F/S).
These will be mentioned if there has been a relatively recent stock split. It will usually mention the date and the ratio of the split.

I would have a read about share splits on the web to get familiar with what they are but essentially they will either lower the float (reverse split) where if you had more shares, they will be converted to fewer shares, but for a higher price. This affects the float - the available shares to trade held by non-affiliates.

This can be useful to know as occasionally there are themes with reverse split stocks with a lower float getting attention.
It can also affect the accuracy of the reported float, especially when it's more recent as they may have not yet been updated.
